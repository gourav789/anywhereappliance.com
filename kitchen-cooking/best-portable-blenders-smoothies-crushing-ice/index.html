What Is a Portable Blender Good for for Ice Blender

Motor power and blade design are the difference between the winners and the rest. You'll need at least 70W up to 180W of sustained torque with stainless steel blades spinning quickly in high RPM to equilibrium to break down the ice cubed chunks without any chunks left. A single speed motor just won't get the job done, look for motors that have a pulse or Auto-iQ program that automatically increases the speed for frozen ingredients.

Battery capacity is also very critical. High mAh ratings (2000mAh or more) provide the possibility of constant power through many blends without inrush torque falling in the middle. A low-powered battery may work just fine with a smoothie, but wilt upon a second help of ice-packed shakes. These elements are most important to those who frequently go to the gym and those traveling or in a tiny apartment or dorm who prefer the smooth and easy factor every time without lugging around a bulky appliance.

How to Clean and Maintain Your Portable Blender

Keeping your blender in peak performing condition only takes less than a minute every day. The best way of doing this is the water and the soap method: fill up halfway with warm water, add one drop of dish soap, and do a 10 second blend cycle. Rinse very well and set without washing, the blades and jar in the sun to dry. Clients are always informed that the motor base should be wiped off with a damp cloth only - never immerse the motor. Charge ready after every few uses just to maintain battery health and you do not blend very hot liquids that might lead to damage of seals over time.

Expert Tips for Getting the Best Results from Portable Blenders

  • Charge after every 5 to 7 uses, avoid running on low battery, store at room temperature, overcharge protection is included in all models.
  • For budget models, such as the solara Blendkwik or Wonderchef Twister, make sure frozen fruit is cut into smaller pieces first, to manage load of ice better and be able to get a smoother result each time.

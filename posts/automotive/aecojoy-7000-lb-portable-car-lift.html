What is the Aecojoy 7000 lb Portable Car Lift?

The Aecojoy is a hydraulic portable car lift designed for home garages. It uses two long lifting beams that slide under the vehicle, similar to the popular QuickJack system. A hydraulic pump raises the car to give you full access underneath for oil changes, brake work, suspension repairs, and inspections.

Key advantages over traditional methods:

  • No permanent installation required
  • Can be rolled out of the way when not in use
  • Works on most cars, SUVs, and light trucks up to 7,000 lbs

This style of lift is ideal for homeowners who want professional-level access without the cost or space requirements of a full 2-post or 4-post lift.

Real-World Performance & Usability

For occasional to regular home use, the Aecojoy performs well. It gives you stable, locked height for comfortable work on oil changes, tire rotations, brake jobs, and exhaust work.

Strengths in daily use:

  • Much safer and faster than floor jacks + jack stands
  • Extension plates help with trucks and wider SUVs
  • Mechanical locks give peace of mind once engaged
  • Relatively quick to set up once you’re familiar with it

Limitations:

  • At 408 lbs, it’s heavy to reposition frequently by yourself
  • The hydraulic system needs occasional maintenance (bleeding air, checking fittings)
  • Maximum height is good but not the tallest in its class

Many users of similar budget 7000 lb lifts report that they become much more confident working under vehicles once they get used to the system.

Safety Features

Safety is the most important factor with any lift. The Aecojoy includes a mechanical locking system with multiple height positions. Once the locks are engaged, the vehicle is held mechanically — not by hydraulic pressure.

Best safety practices:

  • Always use on perfectly level concrete
  • Engage the mechanical locks at your working height
  • Never work under the vehicle without the locks engaged
  • Inspect hoses and fittings before every use
  • Keep the area clear of children and pets

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Strong 7,000 lb capacity at a budget price
  • Truly portable — no floor anchoring needed
  • Mechanical safety locks
  • Comes with useful accessories (extension plates)
  • Much more comfortable than floor jacks
  • Good value for serious DIYers

Cons

  • Heavy (408 lbs) — difficult for one person to move
  • Only 60-day warranty
  • Limited long-term reviews specific to the Aecojoy brand
  • Hydraulic fittings are average quality (common in this price range)
  • Requires level concrete floor

Aecojoy vs QuickJack vs Other Budget Lifts

Bottom line: The Aecojoy offers strong value if you want portable lift capability without spending QuickJack money. However, if you plan to use it very frequently and want better long-term support, the premium option is worth the extra investment.

Who Should Buy It?

Buy the Aecojoy if:

  • You do regular DIY maintenance at home
  • You want better access than jack stands
  • You have a level concrete garage
  • You’re comfortable with basic hydraulic maintenance
  • You want to stay under $1,600

Consider alternatives if:

  • You need daily professional use
  • You want the longest warranty and best support
  • You prefer lighter weight or taller lift height

Maintenance Tips

To get the longest life from your lift:

  • Use quality 46# hydraulic oil
  • Bleed air from the system if lifting feels spongy
  • Inspect hoses and fittings regularly
  • Keep the unit clean and dry
  • Store in a protected area when not in use

Final Verdict

The Aecojoy 7000 lb Portable Car Lift is a solid budget choice for home mechanics. It delivers real usability, good capacity, and useful safety features at a price that undercuts premium brands by a significant margin.

It won’t match the refinement or long-term support of a QuickJack, but for most DIYers who work on their vehicles a few times per month, it represents excellent value.

Rating: 7.8/10 — Recommended for budget-conscious home garage users who understand the trade-offs.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is the Aecojoy 7000 lb lift safe to use? Yes, when used correctly on level concrete and with the mechanical safety locks engaged.

2. How heavy is it really? 408 lbs total. Most people find it easier to move with two people or by using the wheels and ball transfers strategically.

3. Does it come with hydraulic oil? No. You will need to purchase 46# hydraulic oil separately.

4. Can it lift trucks and SUVs? Yes, as long as the vehicle is under 7,000 lbs and you use the extension plates where needed.

5. How does it compare to QuickJack? QuickJack has better build quality, longer warranty, and superior customer support. The Aecojoy is a more affordable alternative that works well for occasional to moderate home use.

6. What is the maximum lift height? 26.8 inches — sufficient for most oil changes, brake work, and inspections.

7. Is assembly difficult? Most users complete assembly in 2–4 hours with basic tools and a helper.

8. Can I use it on my driveway? Only if the driveway is perfectly level and made of solid concrete. Uneven or soft surfaces are unsafe.

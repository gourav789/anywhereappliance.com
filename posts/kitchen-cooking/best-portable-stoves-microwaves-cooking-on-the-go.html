Cramped RV galleys, dingy campsites or tiny dorm's kitchens often require you to choose between dry takeout or no real food. The latest portable stoves and microwaves change that. These small-sized units boil water in less than three minutes, sear up steaks, bake casseroles or even air fry chicken nuggets with gas, 120V power, or even cordless 40V batteries - all while fitting in a backpack or in an RV storage compartment.

Gas vs. Electric vs. Battery: How to Choose Your Power Source for Portable Cooking

Gas and propane stoves provide instant high heat of up to 7.2 kW, and they operate entirely off-grid which is ideal when camping in the national parks or tailgating someone where you need to sear or boil fast.

120V electric models have the power to plug into shore power at RV parks or to the standard power outlet and have the advantages of control and convection and air-fry capabilities not available with traditional gas.

Battery powered are the newest game changer, as they will run from the same 40V packs you already have to run your tools, so that gives you true cord free cooking capability when you can't have generators or hookups.

Safety Tips When Cooking in Small Spaces

  • Always crack a window or crank the RV vent fan when using gas stoves to keep carbon monoxide from building up - get a battery-powered CO detector, if you don't already have one.
  • Keep a minimum of 12-18 inches of clearance area around burners and convective exhaust vents; never keep flammable items such as paper towels, curtains or sleeping bags nearby.

Pro Tips and Warnings for Portable Cooking

Pro Tip: For battery microwaves such as the Makita make sure you start with fully charged 4.0Ah or higher packs - a single 2.0Ah battery only lasts for 10-15 mins at 500W.

Warning: You should never run gas stoves inside of a fully enclosed tent or unvented RV without making sure there is any airflow; carbon monoxide poisoning is silent and deadly.

FAQ

Can we use portable gas stoves inside an RV? Yes - with the proper ventilation and a well-functioning CO detector. Models, including NJ NSD-2, are rated for indoor use when a flow of air is maintained.

How long do Makita 40V batteries last in the cordless microwave? Two 4.0 Ah batteries normally makes available 20-30 mins of 500W cooking power; larger 6.0Ah packs make for a much longer runtime.

Do convection portable microwaves, such as the Contoure, really air fry well? Yes - the SMART air fry mode creates an additional circulation of hot air at a high speed, resulting in crispy outcomes as with countertop air fryers without added oil.

What is the cheapest fuel to use for long term camping? Standard 1 lb canisters of propane are commonly found and quite affordable at Walmart or camping stores - butane is good for stoves of a lighter size such as the GOPEAK Spider.

Are these microwaves allowable on airplanes by TSA? No - lithium batteries over 100Wh (most 40V packs) are only allowed in carry on luggage and not in checked in baggage. Empty and dry unit before flying.

Which is the best stove for high altitude camping? Gas models such as GOPEAK Spider or NJ NSD-2 have a reliable range up to 10,000+ feet with proper flame adjustment and battery microwaves do not care about altitude.

Final Thoughts

The Makita 40V cordless microwave rewards you with hardcore off-grid freedom, without any power, while the NJ NSD-2 two burner gas stove provides the real kitchen experience when you're operating your RV and tailgating. The GOPEAK Spider is the go-to for the backpacker that needs ultra-light packability, the Z.M.C 2-in-1 solves the cold-weather dual function need and the Contoure RV-200S-CON is that ultimate all-in-one solution for the serious RV cook. Pair any of these with the right fuel or batteries and you will be cooking restaurant quality meals anywhere from Yosemite to cross-country road trips.

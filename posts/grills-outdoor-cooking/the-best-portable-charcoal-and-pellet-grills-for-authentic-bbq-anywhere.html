Portable grills provide authentic wood fired barbecue flavor wherever your adventures take you. Tailgating at the stadium, camping deep in the woods, beach picnics or weekend road trips - these small units pack down small with the same smoky, charred results as regular full alternate backyard rigs. Charcoal models provide strong direct heat up to 600degF+ and traditional natural rings of smoke from true lump or briquettes. Pellet models introduce the advantages of pinpoint digital temperature control as well as consistent hardwood smoke from low (180F) and slow smoking to 450F+ searing. Both categories now include lightweight frames of less than 45 pounds, folding legs or carry handles and cooking space of 150 - 425 sq. in. - plenty for 4 - 8 people.

Why Portable Charcoal and Pellet Grills Provide Real BBQ Flavor?

Full-size grills are attached to a location and weigh 50 pounds or more. Portable versions solve that entirely while maintaining actual barbecue performance. Expect 147-300 square inches on most models - enough to for grill burgers for the family or smoke a rack of ribs on a campsite. Charcoal versions do well when searing at high variable temperatures and add real smoke flavor. Pellet versions offer set-it and forget-it aspect with automatic pellet feeding and WiFi monitoring on several models. Availability is good through all Amazon, Home Depot, Tractor Supply, and brands, and most models are in stock year round. Prices vary from entry level at $55 to more delectable $695 builds that rival stationary grills.

How to Choose the Right Portable Grill for Your Needs?

  • Match cooking area to group size - 147-176 square inches for 1-3 people, 200+ square inches for 4-8
  • Weight under 25 pounds is good for backpacking or going out alone; models 35-45 pounds are good for wheels or carts
  • Porcelain enamel or heavy steel construction is rust resistant after repeated outdoor exposure
  • Charcoal requires only a chimney starter and bags of fuel
  • Pellet models need a power source or 12V adapter for extended journeys
  • Factor fuel storage - charcoal will fit in a small bag whereas pellets require hopper space for 4-8 hours of cook time

Pro Tips to Better Portable BBQ Results

  • Natural lump charcoal is easier to ignite and produces cleaner smoke than briquettes
  • Preheat pellet grills 15 minutes so they stabilize in temperature before adding food to them
  • Bring a digital probe thermometer - it is more important for internal temperature in meat than surface temperatures in the grill
  • Pack ash tools and heat-resistant gloves for quick cleanups at camp sites
  • To prevent sticking, oil and burn fire new grills

Warnings and Maintenance of Long-Lasting Performance

  • Never leave charcoal grills unattended because of open fire hazards
  • Dispose of ash only after it is completely cool in a metal container
  • Pellet models require hopper cleaning on a regular basis to prevent dust jams in the auger
  • Wipe grates after each use with a stainless brush
  • Check local fire restrictions before using in dry camping areas - some parks prohibit open charcoal

FAQ

What Size Portable Grill is Best for a Family of Four?

Models with 200+ square inches such as the Weber Jumbo Joe or Traeger Tailgater handle burgers, chicken and sides all together without crowding.

Are Pellet Grills as Portable as Charcoal Grills?

Yes - top ones such as the Green Mountain Trek Prime 2.0 and Z Grills Cruiser fold like a suitcase, weigh less than 45 pounds and have 12V adapters for off-grid use.

How Long Do Good Portable Charcoal Grills Last?

Porcelain-enameled models from Weber, Napoleon and PK Grills routinely pass with flying colors the 5-7 years of weekend use test if ash is removed after each cook, and a cover is used in storage.

Can Small Portable Grills Do Low and Slow Smoking?

Yes. The SNS Slow 'N Sear and Nomad both do 225 F holds for hours indirect using water pans that produce competition quality ribs and brisket anywhere.

What Fuel Should I Bring for Camping Trips Which Last More Than One Day?

Charcoal users require one 8-10 pound bag/day. 10-20 pounds of hardwood pellets carried by the pellet grillers for 8+ hours of running time per full hopper.

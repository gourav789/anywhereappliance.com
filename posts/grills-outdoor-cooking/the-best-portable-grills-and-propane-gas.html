Introduction to the Mobile Grilling Revolution

The paradigm of outdoor cooking has been radically changed, and has moved from simple mobile sources of heat to complex high-performance systems that rival the permanent installation in the backyard. Whether you are setting up at a campsite, enjoying a day at the beach or having an epic tailgate, portable grills give you a chance to enjoy a hot, freshly cooked meal without having to be tied to your home patio. This evolution is brought on by the convergence of advanced material science, precision engineering and a shift in society towards "weeknight BBQ" and the "overlanding" lifestyle - where durability and efficiency are of prime importance.

When it comes to making life a little easier when out in the country or on the road, having the right set up with the best grill and propane available on hand can make all the difference. Manufacturers are now getting their eye driven to units that feature the following: high thermal inertia, even heat distribution, and modularity. Below we are diving into the nitty-gritty of 15 outstanding portable grill and propane gas choices that are not mentioned in our primary archives and give you new and real-time choices for your next adventure.

Expert Buying Guide: How To Recommend Your Next On-The-Go Grill

To provide professional results in different environmental conditions, the portable grill could face huge thermodynamic challenges. Wind, ambient temperature and altitude all affect the efficiency of a burner and stability of cooking surface. When you are looking for the perfect portable grill, you need to look beyond the pleasant aesthetics.

Understanding BTU Output and Cooking Area

Heat output is measured by British Thermal Units (BTUs), but the number of BTUs isn't as important as the density of BTU - what's the heat output in each square inch of cooking surface? High performing portable units are aimed at a BTU density that permits quick pre-heating and quick recovery after opening the lid. When looking at our list match cooking space to the size of your group. A 150 square inch surface is easily adequate for 2 people, any larger than 250 square inches is more appropriate for a family of 4.

Materials and Durability

The key characteristic of a portable grill's longevity is the resistance of thermal cycling and atmospheric corrosion. In coastal or humid situations, stainless steel is the material of choice, in burners and fasteners, because it contains a high chromium and nickel content; this creates a protective layer of oxide. Cast aluminum is another great option for cookboxes as it allows the cookbox to have a high degree of thermal conductivity and heat retention in addition to being resistant to corrosive elements.

Getting the Most Out of Your Grilling Season

Whether you are searing up some steaks in the woods or indulging in a heap of flapjacks sat outside your RV, maintaining your equipment and safely using them is of the utmost importance.

Top 5 Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Portable Grill

  • Preheat your grill entirely for at least 10 to 15 minutes in order to assure the most optimal cooking grates have achieved maximum thermal retention for the best possible sear.
  • Clean your grates with a heavy brush immediately after removing your fare while the metal is still hot as it prevents the accumulation of hard residue.
  • When making your cooking/grilling experience as safe as possible, position your portable grill perpendicular to the wind whenever possible to stop gusts from blowing out your flame or lowering your internal cooking temperatures.
  • Make use of set-ups with two burners which offer a dual zone cooking surface, when searing high meat on the high-heat aspect and let it cook slowly on the unlit or low-heat aspect.
  • De-grease interior of cookbox after each period of use to avoid the build up of corrosive acidicwicklung which can corrode burner tubes over time.

Propane Gas Safety and Transport Guidelines

  • Always check the seals on your propane connections with a solution of water and dish soap; bubbles will be produced if the connection has a leak that needs to be dealt with before igniting it.
  • Always keep propane tanks in an upright position in a well-ventilated place, never in the living space or inside the closed trunk of your vehicle.
  • Never fill a tank above 80% of its volume as this liquid needs additional space inside the cylinder in order to safely expand as the ambient temperature increases.
  • Check for leaks and rust before using it each time, and always follow local refilling and transport guidelines for your tank, to ensure maximum safety.
  • When running an adapter hose to 20 lb tank on grill with built in regulator, make sure that you are running a high pressure hose without second integrated regulator, as you would not want to run into issue of fuel restriction.

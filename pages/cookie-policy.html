Our Cookie Policy describes our use of cookies and other tracking online tools on our website at anywhereappliance.com. What Are Cookies

Cookies are tiny text files which are stored in your machine e.g. your computer, Smartphone or tablets when you shop on a webpage. Your web browser saves these files and websites are able to identify your device and store some information about your visit. The cookies have gained prevalence throughout the internet so as to enable the websites to operate more effectively, enhance end user experience, and give information to the owners of the websites and advertisers.

There are session cookies and persistent cookies, the first type are disposable and the second one stays on your computer until you close your browsers or till a specific time, or until you can manually remove it. There are also first-party cookies and third-party cookies, cookies set by the site you are going on and cookies set by an external service that is running on the site respectively.

How We Use Cookies

Cookies are used by Anywhere Appliance because of the following reasons:

To guarantee the appropriate operation and operation of our site. To know the interaction of the visitors with our site, which pages are the most visited and the duration of the visitor on our site. To enhance our website content and structure and overall user experience depending on visitor behavior. To facilitate the incorporation of the appropriate advertisements using Google AdSense, our third party advertisement partner. To save your preferences and settings so that you can find browsing easy.

Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

Strictly Necessary Cookies are needed in the basic operation of our site. These cookies allow such essential functions as navigation in the pages and entering the closed sections of the site. The website is unable to operate without these cookies. These cookies do not store any information that can be called personally identifiable, and it will not be possible to disable them.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

Performance and Analytics Cookies assist us to determine how many people are visiting our site and what they do on the site such as the number of pages visited, amount of time they spend on the site, and what are the mistakes they make. This data is employed just to better the performance and content of our site. These are cookies that do not recognize you as a person.

Functional Cookies

Functional Cookies make our web site remember what you have done in the past (e.g. what language you prefer or what part of the world you are in) and offers a more personalized experience of the site. The data that is gathered by these cookies does not identify you by name and it cannot be used to trace your activity as a user on the other sites.

Advertising and Targeting Cookies

Our third-party advertising partner Google AdSense uses Advertising and Targeting Cookies to show customized advertisements depending on your preferences and browsing history. These cookies follow you around sites that are part of Google AdSense network and assist in the provision of the relevant ads to you. Third-party cookies are they and are regulated by the own privacy and cookie policies of Google.

We display advertisements on our site, which is based on Google AdSense. Google AdSense is the third party that installs cookies on your gadget so that they can use them to deliver interest based advertisements. Google LLC and its advertisement partners set these cookies, not Anywhere Appliance. The information that these cookies gather is accessible and under the control of the company creating them.

The cookies that are set by Google AdSense can be cookies that store your browsing history, cookies that recognize your browser or device across visits, and cookies that store your interaction with ads like clicks and views.

Cookies Management and Control

With cookies, you are free to eat or refuse. The following are ways by which you can manage your cookies:

Most modern web browsers will enable you to review, control, block or delete cookies through your browser settings. On your browser, you can also be notified whenever a cookie is dropped on your device or you can simply say no cookies. Seek the help documentation of your browser on the management of cookies. The list of common browsers and their cookie management pages consists of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Apple Safari, and Opera.

It is important to note that by disabling or blocking some cookies, you cause certain functionality of our site to not work as it is supposed to or it will interfere with your browsing. Removing the advertising cookies will not take away the ads on our website, but it will show you less relevant ads.

With personalized advertising by Google, you have the option to block this advertising by going to g.co/adsettings through Google opt-out tools. Alternatively, you can choose not to have third-party cookies by going to either one of the following websites: aboutads.info or youronlinechoices.com where you can select industry-wide tools of controlling the use of cookies.

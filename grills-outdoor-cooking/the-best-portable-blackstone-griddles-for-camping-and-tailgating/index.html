Outdoor cooking has evolved. Traditional open-grate grills still have their place, but flat-top griddles have taken over campsites and tailgates for one simple reason - versatility. You can smash burgers, scramble eggs, crunch bacon and turn pancakes - all on one surface - without the flare-ups or uneven heat that plagues wire grates over a fire pit.

The frustration is real. You sit up at a campsite or parking lot to a stadium with a bunch of hungry people. A small camp stove, this stove handles 2 burger at a time. An open fire pit leaves half your food burnt while half of it uncooked. Wind blows out the flame. Cleanup takes forever. Portable Blackstone griddles are the solution for all of those issues. They bring restaurant-style flat top cooking in a package small enough to fit in the back of your car or RV and powerful enough to cook for 2 to 20 people.

Why a Flat Top Griddle is the Ultimate Tailgating and Camping Hack?

A flat-top griddle has four major advantages over a traditional portable grill.

  • First of all, no flare-ups, grease goes down cleanly, not on fire.
  • Second, you prepare breakfast and dinner on the same surface without switching accessories around; eggs and bacon quietly lying in bed with smash burgers.
  • Third, there are built-in wind guards to keep heat constant on windy lake shores or in windy parking lots.
  • Fourth, cleanup is a matter of minutes: scrape while hot, wipe with water and re-oil.

Many models are now made with Blackstone's Omnivore plate technology which heats faster, warps less and reduces propane consumption up to 20% compared with older steel plates.

Important Factors To Consider When Deciding Which Portable Blackstone to Choose

Cooking Space vs. Portability

  • 17-inch 20-25 lbs fits in a backpack or small trunk for single campers or couples
  • 22-inch sweet spot 30-45 lbs 361 square inches large enough for 8-10 burgers
  • 28-inch XL 60+ lbs all the weight but 517-613 square inches for large groups

BTUs and Burners

  • Single burner 12,500 BTU models are heated fast for smaller meals
  • Dual zone 24,000 BTU units allow one end to be set at 350 degrees Fahrenheit for eggs and the other at 500 degrees Fahrenheit for searing
  • Three burner 48,000 BTU 28 in size handle serious crowds

Fuel Source

Most operate off of the standard 1 lb disposable propane canisters for quick trips. For weekend or week-long adventures, include a $25-40 hose adapter along with a 5 lb. or 20 lb tank so you will not have to worry about freezing up mid-meal and refilling contentions.

Legs vs. Table Top

Table top models sit on any picnic table or tailgate. Scissor-leg "On The Go" (OTG) or Flexfold stands fold flat, add wheels on some and eliminate the need of a level surface.

Propane Management on the Go

One 1 lb Green canister burns about 45-90 minutes on a two burner 22 in griddle at medium heat high (this is less than in cold weather when tanks freeze up and the pressure drops). For any travel that you will be taking longer than one meal, invest in a 5 lb or 20 lb tank plus the official Blackstone adapter hose (under $40). You'll cook the whole weekend and not have to trade bottles and put up with the hassle of having a dead tank in the middle of breakfast.

How to Clean and Protect Your Griddle at a Campsite?

  • While the griddle is hot, skim up the grime into the grease cup using a metal scraper.
  • To steam-disgorge the surface with a cup of water, loosening remaining bits, and then wipe with a paper towel or rag.
  • Let it cool completely.
  • Apply a trace of Blackstone seasoning oil or high smoke point oil before packing.
  • Hinges between two covers at the sides stop road dust and grease transfer - this is known as a hard cover or carry bag.

Following these steps each time and your plate will now be rust free for years to come.

Commonly Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I put a regular pot or pan on the Blackstone?

Yes - cast-iron, stainless steel and carbon-steel pans work perfectly. The flat top allows the heat distribution to be even so you can be simmering sauces or boiling pasta right next to your burgers. What to do to avoid an Melodic Medley Burn of injuries to the griddle surface is to avoid non-stick pans.

How long does a 1 lb. tank of propane burn on a 22 inch griddle?

Expect 45 minutes-90 minutes medium-high heat on dual burners. Cold weather makes this dramatically short. Switch contact to 20 lb. tank with adapter hose for any multi meal trip.

Are Blackstone griddles allowed in most campsites?

Yes, at virtually all the campgrounds and private RV parks in state and national parks. These are classified as normal propane appliances. A few voluntarily extremist "no open flame" urban tailgating lots may mandate electric versions - double-check rules for a specific venue.

What size Blackstone griddle to buy for a family of 4?

The 22-inch models offer the best compromise - loads of space for breakfast for six or dinner for eight, but not overpowering for transporting.

How Omnivore plate compares with older Blackstone surfaces?

The Omnivore burns 20-30% hotter, maintains temperature much better in wind, is less likely to warp and extends propane life. Most 2026 models now come with it.
